IMPLEMENTATION OF BASIC GRAPHS

Graph Theory is a branch of mathematics that studies graphs, which are structures used to represent relationships among objects. A graph consists of vertices and edges connecting them. Graphs are widely used in networking, computer science, transportation systems and social networks.

A Complete Graph is a graph in which every pair of distinct vertices is connected by a unique edge. A complete graph with n vertices is represented as Kn.

A Cycle Graph is a graph in which all vertices are connected in a closed chain. Every vertex has degree 2.

A Path Graph consists of a sequence of vertices connected by edges without forming a cycle.

A Complete Bipartite Graph divides vertices into two disjoint sets and every vertex of one set is connected to every vertex of the other set.

These graph structures form the foundation of graph theory and help understand graph properties and connectivity.
