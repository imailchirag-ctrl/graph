GRAPH ISOMORPHISM VERIFICATION

Graph Isomorphism is the study of structural similarity between two graphs. Two graphs are said to be isomorphic if there exists a one-to-one correspondence between their vertices such that adjacency is preserved.

In graph isomorphism, labels are not important; only the graph structure matters. If two graphs have the same number of vertices, edges and connectivity pattern, they may be isomorphic.

Graph isomorphism has applications in chemistry, pattern recognition, computer vision and network analysis. Verification involves checking graph invariants such as degree sequence, number of vertices and number of edges.

Implementation of graph isomorphism helps understand graph equivalence and structural analysis.
