GENERATION OF SUBGRAPHS

A subgraph is a graph formed from a subset of vertices and edges of another graph. Subgraphs help analyze portions of larger graphs.

Types of subgraphs include induced subgraphs, spanning subgraphs and edge-deleted subgraphs.

An induced subgraph contains selected vertices and all edges between them present in the original graph.

A spanning subgraph contains all vertices but only some edges.

An edge-deleted subgraph is formed by removing one or more edges.

Subgraphs are useful in graph decomposition, optimization and network analysis.
